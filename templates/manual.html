📘 Application Manual

Understanding the ShiftSync Algorithm, Settings, & Automated Relief

1. How the Algorithm Works (The Engine)

ShiftSync uses an advanced Dynamic Bottleneck Solver combined with a multi-tier Fairness Sorter. It does not schedule chronologically; instead, it acts like a human solving a puzzle:

  • Dynamic Bottlenecking: It scans the entire month, finds the specific day that is hardest to fill (fewest available engineers), and solves that day first. It recalculates this at every step.
  • Fairness First (Current Month): It prioritizes engineers who currently have the fewest shifts assigned this month to ensure an even spread.
  • Historical Balance: (If enabled) It acts as a tie-breaker by prioritizing engineers who worked the fewest shifts over the past X months.
  • Sat/Sun Balance: It actively tries to ensure engineers get an even mix of Saturdays and Sundays.
  • Flexibility Reward: It prioritizes engineers who provided more available dates. Inflexible engineers cannot "steal" prime shifts from flexible team members.
  • Preference Rank: It respects the exact order (Priority 1, Priority 2, etc.) the engineer submitted via Webex.

2. Customizing Rules (Team Settings)

Team Admins can strictly control the algorithm's boundaries by navigating to the Team Settings page. Changes take effect immediately on the next algorithm run.

🛡️ Algorithm Constraints (Hard Rules)
  • Strict 7-Day Rest:
    If Checked: An engineer working Sunday is strictly forbidden from being assigned to the very next Saturday (ensuring a full 6-day break).
    If Unchecked: The algorithm avoids back-to-back weekends but is allowed to assign them if it's the only way to fill the roster.
  • Allow Same Weekend Shifts:
    If Checked: Engineers can be assigned to work both Saturday and Sunday of the exact same weekend.
    If Unchecked: Strictly limits engineers to a maximum of 1 shift per weekend.
⚖️ Historical Fairness
  • Balance Historical Shifts: When enabled, the algorithm looks back into the database. If two engineers both need a shift this month, the shift goes to the engineer who worked less in the past.
  • Months to look back (1-12): Defines the historical window (e.g., counting all shifts worked in the last 3 months).

3. Leave Blockouts

If an engineer is added to the Leave Blockouts list on the Availability page, the algorithm will absolutely ignore them for that date, even if they accidentally submitted it in their preferences. This is a strict hard constraint.

4. Smart Diagnostics & Troubleshooting

ShiftSync is designed to fail safely. If a perfect roster is mathematically impossible (due to lack of availability or overly strict rules), it will not force-assign engineers to days they didn't prefer.

Instead, it tracks its own calculations and provides a Smart Diagnostic Error telling you exactly which day caused the failure and why.

Example Error:
🚨 Critical Bottleneck: 2026-05-16 (Saturday)
Not enough engineers available.
• 2 engineers were blocked by the 'Same Weekend' rule.
• 1 engineer maxed out their shifts.
Suggestion: Ask engineers to add this date, or relax the constraints in Team Settings.

5. Automated Shift Relief (Callouts)

If an engineer is unable to work a scheduled shift, they can use the Webex Bot to automatically find a replacement without Admin intervention.

⏱️ Rules & Deadlines
  • Shift Hours Only: Relief requests can only be initiated and processed during the team's active shift hours. Timers automatically pause overnight.
  • The Friday Cutoff: The system will hard-stop 2 hours before the shift ends on the Friday preceding the weekend. If no replacement is found by then, it escalates to the Admin.
🤖 How the Bot Finds a Replacement

The bot builds a queue of eligible engineers who preferred the requested date, sorting them by fairness (fewest shifts first). To ensure the shift gets covered, the bot will slowly relax constraints if people decline:

  1. Tier 1: Perfect matches (Respects 7-Day Rest, Same Weekend, and Max Shifts).
  2. Tier 2: Relaxes the 7-Day Rest rule.
  3. Tier 3: Relaxes the Same Weekend rule.
  4. Tier 4: Relaxes the Max Shifts rule.
⏳ The "Concurrent Card" Timer

The bot messages candidates one by one. If Candidate A does not respond within 2 active hours, the bot messages Candidate B. However, Candidate A's card remains open. The first person to click "Accept" gets the shift, and all other cards instantly expire.

🚨 Admin Escalation

If the entire queue declines, or the Friday deadline hits, the bot notifies the Admin with a highly actionable summary:

Manual Intervention Required: Relief Request Failed
Alice requested coverage for Saturday, May 16th, but the automated system could not secure a replacement before the Friday deadline.

❌ Declined: Bob, Charlie
⏳ No Response (Cards Expired): Dave, Eve

💡 AI Suggestion (Engineers to contact manually):
The following engineers did NOT prefer this date, but are the most fair options to ask based on their low shift counts:
1. Frank (Current shifts: 1, Historical: 4)
2. Grace (Current shifts: 2, Historical: 3)
